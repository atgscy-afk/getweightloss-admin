# Documents

## Overview

Members can upload documents to prove identity or verify orders. The **Documents** section is where staff review, approve, or reject these uploads.

## Document Types

| Type | What it's for | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **ID Document** | Proof of identity | Passport, driver's license |
| **Order Proof** | Proof of purchase | Receipt, payment confirmation, order email |

## Viewing Documents

1. Go to **Documents** → **Documents List**
2. See all uploaded documents
3. Filter by:
   - Document type
   - Member
   - Status (pending review, approved, rejected)
   - Date uploaded

## Reviewing a Document

1. Click a document to view it
2. You'll see:
   - **Member info** — who uploaded it
   - **Document** — the image/file
   - **Upload date** — when they submitted it
   - **Current status** — pending, approved, or rejected

## Approving a Document

If the document looks valid:

1. Click **Approve**
2. Add a note (optional): "ID verified," "Receipt confirmed," etc.
3. Click **Save**

The member is notified their document was approved.

## Rejecting a Document

If the document is:
- Blurry or illegible
- Expired
- Wrong document type
- Doesn't match member's profile

**To reject:**

1. Click **Reject**
2. Add a **reason** (visible to member):
   - "Image is blurry — please resubmit"
   - "Passport expired — please provide current ID"
   - "Receipt doesn't match order details"
3. Click **Save**

The member is notified and can resubmit a corrected document.

## Document Status Workflow

```
Pending (just uploaded)
    ↓
    ├→ Approved ✅ (verified, member can proceed)
    │
    └→ Rejected ❌ (member must resubmit with corrections)
        ↓
    Pending (resubmitted by member)
        ↓
    Approved ✅
```

## Why Documents Matter

- **ID verification** — confirms customer is who they claim to be
- **Fraud prevention** — reduces chargebacks and disputes
- **Order verification** — proof they made a legitimate purchase
- **Compliance** — some jurisdictions require document verification

> **Tip:** Be respectful and clear in rejection reasons. Members are trying to help; they just may have submitted the wrong file or a blurry photo.
